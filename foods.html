Foods to Try in Nepal

Nepali cuisine is a flavorful journey through its diverse culture and geography. From the mountains to the plains, each dish tells a story. Here are some must-try dishes during your visit:

Momo

What: Steamed or fried dumplings filled with meat or vegetables.
Why Try: Nepal‚Äôs most popular street food, often served with spicy dipping sauce.

Dal Bhat

What: Lentil soup (dal) and rice (bhat), served with vegetables, pickles, and curry.
Why Try: The daily staple food of most Nepalese‚Äînutritious and filling.

Sel Roti

What: A homemade, deep-fried rice flour ring/doughnut.
Why Try: Popular during festivals, crispy outside and soft inside.

Yomari

What: Rice flour dough stuffed with sweet molasses or sesame paste, steamed to perfection.
Why Try: A Newari delicacy traditionally eaten during winter festivals, especially Yomari Punhi.

Chatamari

What: A thin rice flour crepe, usually topped with minced meat, eggs, veggies ‚Äî then cooked until crispy.
Why Try: Often called "Nepali Pizza" - a Newari cuisine that combines crisp texture with rich, spiced toppings.

Dhido

What: Thick porridge-like dish made by stirring buckwheat or millet flour in hot water, eaten with leafy greens or meat curry.
Why Try: A nutritious dish rooted in rural Nepalese culture, especially loved in the hilly and mountainous regions.
